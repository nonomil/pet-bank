Use case: ui-mockup
Asset type: high-fidelity desktop web application concept for a children's learning dashboard
Primary request: Create a polished straight-on desktop screenshot concept for the learning tab of a bright children's pet growth application. The main navigation problem is solved by a tall scrollable local sidebar inside the learning page, allowing many learning categories, while the right side remains a card-based learning workspace.
Scene/backdrop: full browser content only, no laptop frame, no photographed screen, no perspective distortion; 1536 by 1024 landscape composition.
Composition/framing: a slim global app header across the top, then a two-column learning workspace. Left column is about 250 pixels wide and visually independent, with a compact pet avatar/header at the top, twelve evenly spaced vertical navigation rows with consistent simple line icons, small secondary placeholder lines, one active row highlighted in fresh green and sky blue, and a subtle scrollbar. Right column fills the remaining width with a compact learning overview header, one horizontal progress area, a small row of progress metrics, then a clear three-column grid of nine medium learning category cards. Keep all important content inside safe margins.
Right-side card subjects: early childhood bridge learning with an open workbook and pencil; picture-book reading with an open illustrated book; Chinese literacy and pinyin represented by brush, speech bubbles, and flashcards without actual characters; beginner math with counting beads and geometric blocks without numbers; English story learning with headphones and story cards without letters; block-world vocabulary with a bright voxel grass block and headphones but no copyrighted logo; learning games with a small joystick; learning websites with a globe; printable worksheets with paper and printer. Use distinct illustrations but one consistent visual system.
Style/medium: high-fidelity modern web UI mockup, clean 2D product design with subtle children's editorial illustrations, crisp CSS-like surfaces, bright and technologically fresh, friendly for children aged 5 to 8, aligned with a polished education dashboard rather than a marketing page.
Color palette: warm white and very pale mint base, forest green primary, clear sky blue secondary, sunflower yellow and coral accents, small neutral charcoal text-placeholder bars; balanced multi-color palette, no dominant purple, no dark navy theme, no muddy beige, no vintage or pixel-art treatment.
Materials/textures: mostly flat clean surfaces, 8 pixel card corners, thin visible borders, subtle soft shadows, stable card dimensions, no glass blur, no nested cards.
Text: no embedded text. Use clean blank label areas and short neutral placeholder bars only so real Chinese labels can be overlaid later.
Constraints: left vertical sidebar must be the strongest navigation signal and show at least twelve distinct rows; active row must be obvious; right workspace must show progress plus category cards; keep the layout practical and implementable in HTML and CSS; strong text contrast zones; generous but efficient spacing; consistent icon sizing; no overlapping elements.
Avoid: no text, no letters, no numbers, no Chinese characters, no pinyin, no fake writing, no logos, no watermark, no emoji icons, no giant hero, no decorative orbs, no heavy gradients, no pill-shaped text controls, no 3D laptop mockup, no angled camera, no photographic moire, no excessive rounded cards, no nested cards, no crowded top navigation.
